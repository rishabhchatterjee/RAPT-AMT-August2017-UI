Definition

In this task, you will be labeling for hedging. Hedging is a rhetorical strategy used to lessen the impact of an utterance, as in “He’s sort of nice”, or the full force of a speech act, as in “I must ask you to stop doing that”


Different sorts of hedging:

- Apologies - When used to soften the directness of an utterance, as in “Sorry, it’s not correct.”.
- Extenders - Words used to indicate uncertainty by referring to vague categories, as in “You have to call her or something.” (also: “...and stuff”, “...and that”, “...and so forth”)
- Qualifiers - Qualifying words used to reduce the intensity or certainty of an utterance, as in “You just have to run.” (also: “a little, maybe, just, actually, sort of, kind of, some, somewhat, pretty much, fairly”)
- Subjectivizer - Words that make an utterance seem more subjective to reduce the intensity or certainty, as in “I think you should do that.” (also: “I believe”, “I guess”, “I don’t know”)


During the annotation phase, if the sentence is not finished and there isn’t enough to decide on, choose “none”.

Examples (Hedging vs. Non-Hedging)
