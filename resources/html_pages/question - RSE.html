Definition

In this task, you will be labeling for references to shared experience. Refer to shared experience (RSE) is applied to the verbal expressions by which a person refers to an event, activity, or experience that was shared by that person and the other person in the conversation. Shared experience must be something that was experienced together.

Things that are NOT referring to shared experience:

- “That's cool, I play volleyball too!” – just because they play the same game, does not mean they played the game together – it’s the togetherness that makes it shared experience.
- “This problem is so hard. I’m just going to skip it.” – This is not RSE because it is a current running event.
- If they don’t finish their sentence and there isn’t enough to decide on, choose “none”


Examples (Refer to shared experience vs. Not refer to share experience)

I remember you helped me with a problem like this before.
This problem is so hard.

That soccer game we saw last week was so lame.

I like those because we actually work together on them.
This is super awkward.

I didn't think we were gonna figure that one out!
