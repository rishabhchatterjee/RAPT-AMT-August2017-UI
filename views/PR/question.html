Introduction

In this HIT, you will label a set of sentences with a particular type of conversational move. We will first describe the conversational move and give you a set of examples. Before you begin labeling the actual sentences, you will take a quiz to ensure you understand how to recognize the particular conversation move described. After passing this quiz, you will be redirected to the task.


Definition

In this task, you will be labeling for praise. Praise is the expression of a positive judgement of :
- a product - what other persons make or do (or what they have made or done in the past).
- a behavior - how other persons behave, (or how they have behaved in the past).
- an attribute, who other persons are (or were in the past).
It is important to note that in our annotations of these transcripts we are not interested in praise of a third party (e.g. “my mother is really smart”) but praise of the other person in the conversation (e.g. “you’re really smart!”).


Things that are NOT praise:

- “That’s right” – this is just an acknowledgement of correctness - and is a statement of fact, not an evaluation.
- “You scored 60%!” – this is also just an acknowledgement – and is a statement of fact, not an evaluation.
- “You can do it!” – this is encouragement, not praise.
- During the annotation phase, if the sentence is not finished and there isn’t enough to decide on, choose “none”.


Examples (Praise vs. Non-Praise)

The penguins aced last game!
