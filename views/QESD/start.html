    • QESD Non-QESD

    Note: A question that elicits self-disclosure (QESD) is when people ask questions to which the responses are expected to be self-disclosures. Self-Disclosures reveal deep, personal, or enduring information about ourselves to other people that wouldn’t typically be publicly known. So, for example, "What is your biggest fear?” IS a question to elicit self-disclosure because it expects an intimate or personal answer in return. “Do you know what time it is?” is NOT a question to elicit self-disclosure because its answer will not reveal any deep, personal or enduring information about its author.
    Things that are NOT elicit self-disclosure:
    - “Do you have a pencil?” - Just because it is a question does not mean it is a QESD. Here, the answer expected does not deliver any intimate or personal information about its author.
    - “Do you think Tom has a girlfriend?” - Here, the answer to this question is an opinion, not a personal information.
    - During the annotation phase, if the sentence is not finished and there isn’t enough to decide on, choose “none”.
