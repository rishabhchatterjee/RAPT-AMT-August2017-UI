Definition

In this task, you will be labeling for self-disclosure. Self-disclosure is when people reveal deep, personal, or enduring information about themselves to other people that wouldn’t typically be publicly known. So, for example “I’m terrible at math” IS a self-disclosure because it tells you about the person’s enduring preferences, but “I think we have to do some math now” is NOT a self-disclosure because it isn’t deep, personal, or enduring.


Things that are NOT self-disclosure:

- Just because they say “I think” doesn’t mean it’s self-disclosure. Sometimes people “hedge”, or show they’re uncertain, by starting a sentence with “I think”.
- If they don’t finish their sentence and there isn’t enough to decide on, choose “none”.
- Sharing opinions about other people: “Ms. Jones has the worst dresses” is NOT self-disclosure.


Examples (Self-Disclosure vs. Non-Self-Disclosure)

I am always cold.
It is cold right now.

I want to study biology when I go to college.
Ms. Smith is the worst teacher.

I’m always so awkward.
This is awkward.

I play hockey.
Your team played really well today!

I like these kind of problems.
You did great on the last test!


Note: Stating one’s preferences is a type of self-disclosure we are interested in, and this should be coded with neutral valence. Preferences can be usually identified by words such as “like”, “dislike”, “don’t like”, “hate”, etc.
