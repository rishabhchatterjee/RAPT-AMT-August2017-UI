    Note: Social norm violations are behaviors that go against culturally acceptable conventions or social rules.

    Different sorts of social norm violations:
    - Breaking the conversational rules - This includes violation of general social norm, such as “I’m a Jew” or "Chill out bro!". It also includes inappropriate topics (e.g. violence and sex - "hanging dead bodies from the ceiling", “I’m gonna put my nut sack on your drumset” etc.) and things you are not expected to say to someone you are not already intimate with.
    - Face threatening acts - This includes criticizing, teasing, or insulting things such as personality (e.g. “You’re a weirdo”), personal habits (e.g. “You stink”), work habits (“That’s not going to get you very far in life!”) or interests (“I can’t believe you like World of Warcraft!”) among other things. Sarcastic remarks still count.
    - Referring to specific violations of social norms - This includes referring by oneself, as in “I don’t burp in front of people” or by other people, as in “Come on! Don’t burp in front of me!”.
